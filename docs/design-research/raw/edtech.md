## 1. Coursera
- **URLs:** coursera.org | coursera.org/learn/[course-slug] | coursera.org/degrees
- **Category:** MOOC / university partnerships / degree programs
- **Audience:** Adult learners, professionals, university students
- **Palette:** Coursera blue (~#0056D2, deep saturated blue) as primary. White background default, lots of whitespace.
- **Typography:** Source Sans Pro historically; recently shifted to a custom sans (clean geometric). Vibe: corporate-academic, trustworthy.
- **Layout patterns:** Top horizontal nav. Course pages use left content + right rail (enrollment CTA, syllabus). Card grid for catalog. Week-by-week chapter navigator.
- **Steal:** Week/module progress accordion with checkmarks; specialization stacked card with multi-course progress; partner logo bar (university crests) for instant credibility.
- **Relevance:** 4/5 — strong reference for credibility patterns, weak for premium aesthetic.

## 2. Duolingo
- **URLs:** duolingo.com
- **Palette:** Duo green ~#58CC02, feathers blue ~#1CB0F6, gold streak ~#FFC800.
- **Typography:** Feather Bold custom rounded sans. Playful, mascot-driven.
- **Layout:** Vertical skill-tree as main UI. Left sidebar (Learn, Practice, Leaderboards, Quests, Shop, Profile).
- **Steal:** Streak counter, XP progress bar, daily goal ring, skill tree node states.
- **Reject:** Mascots, candy palette, gamification aesthetic — childish for ESCP.
- **Relevance:** 3/5 — steal mechanics, reject aesthetic.

## 3. Khan Academy + Khanmigo
- **URLs:** khanacademy.org
- **Palette:** Khan green ~#14BF96, supportive blues, subject-coded colors.
- **Typography:** Lato. Warm-academic.
- **Layout:** Left sidebar course tree (unit → lesson → exercise). Video + transcript + practice in tabs. Mastery bars.
- **Steal:** Mastery levels (Attempted → Familiar → Proficient → Mastered) per skill. Khanmigo: chat panel pinned right side of lesson, context-aware. Energy points.
- **Relevance:** 5/5 — mastery model and AI tutor placement directly portable.

## 4. Brilliant
- **URLs:** brilliant.org
- **Palette:** Dark default — deep navy near-black bg (~#0E1116), bright accents (yellow ~#FFD60A, electric blues/purples).
- **Typography:** Custom sans, geometric, slight personality. Heading more expressive.
- **Layout:** Mobile-first feel even on desktop. Course = stack of interactive lessons, each lesson = swipeable cards. Minimal chrome.
- **Steal:** One-concept-per-card lessons. Interactive math/graph widgets inline. "Today" daily challenge home.
- **Relevance:** 4/5 — premium dark aesthetic and interactive widgets are strong references.

## 5. MasterClass
- **URLs:** masterclass.com
- **Palette:** Black/near-black ~#000/#0A0A0A, white text, red accent.

- **Layout:** Hero video autoplay full-bleed. Catalog = full-bleed image cards with instructor portrait. Lesson page = video + chapter list + workbook.
- **Steal:** Instructor-as-hero card (→ "Professor-as-hero"). Cinematic course landing with autoplay trailer. Workbook PDF integrated.
- **Relevance:** 5/5 — single strongest reference for "premium EdTech that feels luxurious."

## Synthesis: 5 Strongest Design Directions Across EdTech

**1. Premium dark editorial (MasterClass, Brilliant, Pluralsight, Obsidian).** Most credible "this is worth paying for" signal in EdTech. Dark navy/near-black bg, restrained accent, serif or expressive headings + clean sans body, generous spacing. Reads as serious tool, not free-tier. Combine with confident European-academic accent (deep teal, oxblood, gold) to avoid silicon-valley-purple cliché.

**2. Calm minimal Notion-aesthetic light mode.** Off-white bg, near-black text, restrained single accent, Inter, whitespace, slash-commands, toggle blocks. Modern without trying hard. Worth offering as light counterpart to direction 1.

**3. Familiar LMS shell, radically better interior (Canvas-shaped, MasterClass-feeling).** Faculty/students expect left icon rail and modules-style course view. Don't fight that mental model. Adopt familiar shell, deliver radically better content + AI rail (Khanmigo) + planner.

**4. Mastery + paths, not just completion.** Replace "% complete" with real mastery: Attempted → Familiar → Proficient → Mastered, visualized per skill. Stack into Paths for multi-course curricula. More credible to faculty than progress bars.

**5. AI tutor pinned right rail, context-aware (Khanmigo).** Canonical pattern. Persistent panel, knows current lesson, can quiz/explain/generate practice. UNIFLOW should ship this pattern exactly.

**Recommended direction:** Dark editorial premium (MasterClass × Notion × Pluralsight) + Canvas-shaped familiar shell + Khanmigo AI rail + Khan mastery model. Reject Duolingo gamification aesthetic; borrow its mechanics quietly. Reject Coursera/Udemy mass-market feel.
